Bharat Mobility Global Expo 2025 Highlights

The Bharat Mobility Global Expo 2025 is currently underway, showcasing significant advancements in the mobility sector from January 17 to 22, 2025. Here are the key developments and highlights from the event:

Product Launches and Innovations

  • Over 90 Product Launches: The expo has seen an impressive array of product launches, including 56 new products unveiled on the second day alone, featuring contributions from 22 leading brands across various mobility segments
  • Electric Vehicles (EVs): Notable launches include: VinFast India introduced its first electric SUVs, the VF 7 and VF 6.BYD India launched the @BYD SEALION 7 eSUV and showcased its innovative Yangwang U8

Noteworthy Exhibits

  • Flying Taxi: Shunya, India’s first flying taxi, was introduced by Sarla Aviation , representing a significant advancement in urban mobility solutions.
  • Electric Vehicles: VinFast Auto India launched its first electric SUVs, the VF 7 and VF 6, marking its entry into the Indian EV market. BYD India presented the BYD SEALION 7 eSUV and showcased the SEALION 6 and Yangwang U8. Vayve Mobility introduced India’s first solar electric car, Eva, with battery options ranging from 9 kWh to 18 kWh.
  • Electric Buses: JBM Electric Vehicles unveiled four new electric buses, including the luxury Galaxy coach, the intercity Xpress, and the medical mobile unit e-MediLife.
  • Commercial Vehicles: Omega Seiki Mobility launched the M1KA 1.0 electric truck and the upgraded M1KA 3.0.EKA Mobility showcased India's largest electric commercial vehicle portfolio, including multiple bus and truck models.
  • Motorcycles and Scooters: Suzuki Motorcycle India debuted the e-ACCESS, its first electric scooter, alongside the all-new Access 125. Hero MotoCorp launched several new models including its first flex-fuel motorcycle, the Hero HF Deluxe.
  • Advanced Technologies: Companies like ZF introduced advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and fleet management platforms.

Key Advancements in Battery Technology

  • BorgWarner's LFP Battery Systems: BorgWarner presented its Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) battery systems, which feature a modular architecture based on blade cell technology. These batteries offer high energy density, adaptability for various vehicle types, and enhanced reliability and safety. They are designed to support electrified commercial vehicles, including buses and trucks, and are equipped with advanced electronics and software for future-proofing.
  • Olectra's Blade Battery Technology: Olectra Greentech Limited unveiled its Blade Battery, developed in collaboration with BYD. This battery provides 30% more energy storage, allowing electric buses to travel up to 500 kilometers on a single charge. It boasts a compact design that enhances vehicle stability and has a lifespan exceeding 5,000 charge cycles, making it a durable option for electric mobility.
  • Innovative Charging Solutions: The expo highlighted advancements in charging efficiency and energy density across various battery technologies. Companies showcased their latest solutions aimed at improving the overall performance of electric vehicles (EVs) while reducing charging times.
  • Modular Battery Packs: Several exhibitors, including Advik Hi-Tech Pvt Ltd , showcased modular battery packs designed for flexibility in energy storage applications. These innovations align with the growing demand for scalable solutions in the electric mobility sector.

Government Support and Vision

  • Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated the expo, highlighting India's commitment to transforming its mobility landscape through initiatives focused on green technology and sustainable practices. He emphasized the importance of the automotive sector in achieving a "Viksit Bharat" by 2047
  • The event aims to unite the entire mobility value chain under one umbrella, facilitating collaboration between industry stakeholders and government bodies

Semco Infratech Pvt Ltd is showcasing its advanced lithium-ion battery assembly and testing solutions emphasizing precision and efficiency in battery production. Their equipment is designed to meet the growing demands of the electric vehicle market.

This year's Bharat Mobility Global Expo is not just a showcase of vehicles but a platform for discussing the future of mobility in India, with an emphasis on innovation, sustainability, and global collaboration.
