Comprehensive Low-Voltage Battery Maintenance Guidelines for Vehicles

Here are some battery maintenance guidelines for Vehicles!

  • For vehicles without intelligent charging, it's recommended to disconnect the negative battery terminal.
  • Inventory vehicles lacking intelligent charging should be recharged every six months to maintain optimal battery health.
  • Vehicles with intelligent charging systems should ensure the power battery maintains a minimum 40% charge when stored. If below this threshold, immediate charging is advised.
  • Batteries in stock vehicles unused for over a year, without adhering to the above maintenance, must be replaced and safely disposed of.
  • In cases of over-discharged batteries (e.g., requiring external power to start), immediate recharging is necessary. Reuse of such batteries without proper recharging is prohibited.
  • Before leaving the warehouse, thorough checks should be conducted to ensure batteries are securely installed, and free of leaks, cracks, or dirt.
  • Before departure, verify that the battery voltage is not below 12.4V. If below this level even after regular charging, consider replacing the battery.
  • Upon receiving vehicles, confirm the battery is securely installed and has sufficient voltage for starting.
  • Before storage, ensure all electrical components are turned off, and remotely lock the vehicle, keeping the key at least 5 meters away.
  • Test drive vehicles should undergo daily battery voltage checks, with recharging promptly if below 12.4V.
  • Exhibition vehicles should be equipped with a specialized external DC-stabilized power supply.
  • Before customer handover, adherence to specific battery recharge guidelines is imperative.
  • It's advised to refrain from installing certain equipment in the warehouse. If necessary, use equipment with its power supply, avoiding connections to the vehicle network or power supply.

For OTA upgrades and software brushing, ensure the battery is fully charged before commencing, and verify it meets voltage requirements post-upgrade. Follow specific charging methods for different battery types and observe precautions for battery replenishment to prevent accidents. Finally, a specific vehicle voltage test method is outlined after the vehicle is turned off, providing a comprehensive approach to battery maintenance and safety.
