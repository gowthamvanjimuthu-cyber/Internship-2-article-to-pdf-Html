India's Electric Vehicle Revolution: A Race to Dominate the Battery Market

Union Government is Planning to soon start a bidding process for an approximately Rs 8,000 crore ($960 million) incentive scheme for the production of electric vehicle batteries. The news, as reported by Bloomberg, citing sources, said, “The scheme will mandate successful bidders to establish cutting-edge chemistry battery plants, collectively generating a capacity of 20 gigawatt-hours in the coming month.”

As India takes bold steps towards a greener future, it is crucial for industry stakeholders, policymakers, and investors to collaborate and seize this opportunity. By leveraging India's vast potential and nurturing a thriving ecosystem for EV battery manufacturing, the nation can become a global hub for clean energy innovation.

It remains to be seen how this program unfolds and the impact it will have on India's EV industry. However, one thing is certain: India's commitment to accelerating the production of electric vehicle batteries is a testament to its determination to shape a sustainable and prosperous future.

This is a significant step in India's push to promote electric vehicles and reduce its reliance on fossil fuels. The incentive scheme is expected to attract major investments in the battery manufacturing sector and help India establish itself as a global leader in EV technology. The production of 20 gigawatt-hours of batteries will be enough to power millions of electric vehicles, and it will also create thousands of new jobs. This is a positive development for India's economy and its environment.

Still, there are two sides to a coin and we must see both to win the toss. I remember mentioning this thing in one of my recent articles too. (China). Having a side-by-side comparison will let us know India’s position at the world level and then we can start the quest for solutions. Global production for lithium-ion batteries was at more than 7 GWh and India was crippling at 0.9 GWh. 

As graphite is a major component of lithium-ion batteries almost the whole world is dependent on Chinese imports, and it becomes hard to even think of self-reliance. The Domain is highly affected by geopolitical and economic factors. Also, big players can hinder the raw material supply and stop countries from increasing their production. We have recently seen the news about China’s decision to save graphite for its domestic industries. Approximately 900 gigawatt-hours, or 77 percent of the world's total Lithium Ion batteries are manufactured in China, which is also home to six of the top ten battery producers worldwide. China's vertical integration throughout the rest of the EV supply chain, from mining the metals to making the EVs, is what accounts for its dominance in batteries.

India should strongly consider capitalizing on the growth of the global as well as domestic lithium-ion battery industry. By investing in Research and Development, India can position itself as a key player in this rapidly expanding market. Additionally, exploring alternative materials for electric vehicle batteries can provide an edge in terms of sustainability and cost-effectiveness. Embracing such initiatives will not only boost India's economy but also contribute to the global shift towards cleaner and greener transportation options.
